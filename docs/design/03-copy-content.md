# 3. Copy + Content Direction

> How we speak to a new group leader: clear, warm, practical, consistent with
> Practicing the Way. Copy is not the primary deliverable (the team has writers), but
> content direction and messaging hierarchy are.

## The governing line

> **Communication should promote calm, quiet confidence, even when not everything is
> known and not everything is done.**

Everything below serves that sentence. The guiding principle for *what* we say is
**"the next right step."**

## The voice, as a usable filter

Run every line through this:

- **Calm, warm, invitational, not directive.**
- **Plain sentences with quiet depth.** First-person plural ("we"). Sentence case.
- **Normalize not-knowing**, the signature move for this audience: *you don't need to
  have it all figured out to begin well.*
- **Room to celebrate.** Some moments should champion the leader, not only reassure
  them. Calm is the baseline, not a ceiling on warmth.
- **No hype, no urgency, no guilt, no jargon.**
- **No em dashes.**
- **One ask** per message.
- **Name the action, never the AI.** No "assistant," no "bot."

## Two hierarchies

**A. Selection, *which* one thing (across messages).** We say the single thing that
most serves the leader *now, in this channel.*
- **Primary** = the one next right step *(do)*. Always exactly one. Confidence before
  invitation.
- **Secondary** = the conversation *(refine)*. A quiet link to go deeper, never a
  competing ask.
- **Tertiary** = a reflection / hint / did-you-know *(season)*. Optional, low-noise,
  easily ignored.

**B. Within a single message:** **what to do → what to know → how to feel**, where
*how to feel* is almost always carried by **tone, not stated.** We don't write "feel
calm"; the restraint makes them calm.

## The skeleton

> One warm line of acknowledgement (often celebratory) → the one thing → a beat that
> **lowers the weight or lifts them up** (it's small and finishable, or: you are more
> ready than you think) → single action → (quiet secondary).

Consistency across email, in-app, and conversation comes from reusing this shape.

## The pre-seeded conversation mechanism

The email's secondary invitation links into the conversation with a **pre-filled message
authored as the leader** (a `?q=` style query param). The conversation therefore opens
with *the leader's* words already said, and the experience responds. It does **not** open
with the tool greeting or introducing itself.

## Canonical examples (ROUGH DRAFTS, coloring to be revisited)

> These demonstrate the system and double as the real strings the prototypes will use.
> Tone/coloring is provisional: lean more celebratory in places, keep stripping anything
> that feels off-voice. Em dashes removed.

**① First email, Week 1 (primary: confidence, not invitation)**
> **Subject:** A calm place to begin
> **Preview:** One short step before your group gathers.
>
> Hi Sarah,
> You've said yes to leading your group through the Practicing the Way Course. That is a
> meaningful, generous thing, and you don't need to have it all figured out to begin well.
> There's just one thing worth doing first. Not inviting anyone yet, not planning
> sessions. Just watching **session one** of the leader training, about six minutes,
> so the shape of what's ahead feels familiar.
> **[ Begin session one ]**
> Not sure where your head's at? *Talk it through*
> Peace to you as you start,
> The Practicing the Way team

**② In-app first visit, centered arrival (expectations)**
> Welcome, Sarah.
> Over the next few weeks we'll help you feel ready to lead, one step at a time, never all
> at once. We'll begin with you, then your group.
> **[ Begin ]**

**③ Conversation opening, pre-seeded as the leader (`?q=`)**
> *Leader's pre-filled message:* "I'd like to talk through getting ready for my first
> session."
> *The experience responds, grounded in that, and asks one warm, open question back.*

**④ Confidence reflection, post-launch ceremony**
> You've led your first session. However it went, that took courage.
> *How are you feeling about leading, now that you've begun?*
> `still finding my feet ----●----- steady`
> → *"That's honest, and it's enough. We'll meet you there."*

**⑤ The "no movement" nudge, kind, not guilty**
> No rush, Sarah. The course is here whenever you're ready, and your first session with
> your group is the only date that matters. When you've got a few quiet minutes, session
> one is a good place to begin.
> **[ Begin session one ]**
