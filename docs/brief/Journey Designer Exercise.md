# **Design Exercise: New Group Leader Onboarding Experience**

## **Context**

A new person comes to the Practicing the Way platform and creates an account for the first time.

During sign-up, we learn that they are a group leader, or that they intend to lead a group through the Practicing the Way Course. We also learn that they plan to begin with their group in about three weeks.

Your assignment is to design the onboarding experience that should happen next.

The goal is to help this person feel clear, encouraged, and prepared to begin leading. We want the experience to help them understand what matters most, know where to start, and take a few helpful next steps before their group begins.

## **Source Material**

Please spend time orienting yourself to the Practicing the Way [website](https://www.practicingtheway.org/getting-started), [The flagship Course](https://launch.practicingtheway.org/resources/practicing-the-way-course), and group leader [training videos](https://launch.practicingtheway.org/training?type=discussion_host) found within the platform.

The discussion host and coordinator training videos are not meant to be summarized one by one. Instead, use them to understand what a group leader is being invited into and what kind of guidance would be most helpful as they prepare.

## **Your Assignment**

Design an onboarding flow or experience for this new group leader.

This could take the form of onboarding screens, dashboard modules, a checklist, an in-app guide, a tutorial, supporting emails, or another approach you think would best serve the user. You do not need to use all of those formats. We want to see how you would think about the experience.

Please spend no more than 3–4 hours on this exercise. We are more interested in your thinking, tradeoffs, and instincts than in a fully polished final product.

## **Please Include**

### **1\. Experience Overview**

Show the overall shape of the onboarding experience.

Include:

* What happens first  
* What the user is invited to do next  
* How the experience prepares them over the next three weeks  
* Any key moments, states, or touchpoints  
* What belongs in the platform versus what might happen through email or another channel

### **2\. Visual Design**

Include visual design for at least one key part of the experience.

This could be a screen, dashboard state, checklist, onboarding flow, email, or other touchpoint. It does not need to be polished production-ready design, but it should be clear enough for us to understand your aesthetic and UI skillset.

You may include additional sketches, wireframes, or visual directions for other parts of the flow.

### **3\. Copy and Content Direction**

Include the key copy or content direction for the experience.

This should show how you would speak to a new group leader in a way that is clear, warm, practical, and consistent with Practicing the Way. Copy is not the primary focus here \- we have writers on the team \- but clear content direction and messaging hierarchy are important.

### **4\. Rationale**

Briefly explain your thinking.

Please include:

* Why you designed the experience this way  
* What you learned from the facilitator training videos  
* What you think a new group leader most needs at this moment  
* What you chose not to include  
* How you would know whether this onboarding experience is working

## **What We’re Looking For**

We are not looking for a perfect final product.

We are looking for clear thinking, user empathy, strong writing, good product instincts, and the ability to turn formation content into a simple digital experience that helps someone take the next right step.

Prayers for peace and creativity as you design\!
