# Visual Design

The look follows the brand exactly, because the aesthetic is itself an argument for calm. Plus Jakarta Sans standing in for Satoshi, at regular weight even for headings. Warm off-white surfaces, never pure white. Near-black text. A single salmon accent, and no second accent in any one context. Generous whitespace, soft diffuse shadows, motion that fades and rises rather than snaps.

> An invitation, not a notification.

It should read like a premium editorial spiritual journal, not a tech product and not a church website.

## One principle, three surfaces

Every prompt the leader meets is an invitation, not a notification. That means it is singular, one element and one action, with nothing competing or stacking. It is placed, not alarming: no badges, no red dots, no unread counts, no urgency. It moves calmly. What makes it read as an invitation is the restraint, the placement, and the copy, not a recurring brand mark stamped on everything.

The same principle plays at three intensities.

- **Centered arrival.** For threshold moments: the very first visit, the post-launch reflection. A moment worth pausing for.
- **Inline card.** The everyday next right step on the workspace. Part of the room.
- **Corner invitation.** Ambient, low-stakes nudges. The next live session is Thursday. The lightest touch.

## The core artifact: email into conversation

The priority build is one coupled flow, because it demonstrates the most from a single piece. The email's quiet secondary link deep-links into a conversation that is already pre-seeded with the leader's own words.

**The email is a letter, not a campaign.** A single narrow column on warm off-white. A regular-weight headline and two or three short paragraphs. One salmon button for the single next step. Beneath it, one quiet text link onward. Warmly signed. The restraint is the aesthetic.

**The conversation is not a chatbot.** No avatar, no robot glyph, no "AI is typing," never labelled "AI Chat." A calm reading column with messages set as warm typography and room to breathe. The input is a single quiet field. Suggested next steps appear as cards. Delivered resources appear as inline cards. The whole thing is titled by the action, *"Prepare for your first session,"* never by the technology.

## The language rule

Name the experience by its function. Never personify it, never brand it as "an AI." The container is a conversation. The entry point is the action. The model underneath is plumbing the leader never needs to think about.
