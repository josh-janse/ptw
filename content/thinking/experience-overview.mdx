# Experience Overview

A new leader has just said yes to running the Practicing the Way Course with their group in about three weeks. The first training video can leave them feeling the weight of it. So the whole onboarding is built as one thing: a counterweight to overwhelm.

> One next step, never many. Confidence before connection.

Two principles carry everything. First, reduce every moment to the single next right thing. Second, steady the leader before opening the circle, because being responsible for other people before you feel steady yourself is what produces the overwhelm in the first place.

The disposition we are aiming for is precise: calm, prepared, surrendered, confident. The quiet posture of *"I do not need to have the answer. I am a facilitator."*

## What happens first

The opening is deliberately small. A genuine, unhurried beat of encouragement, never hype. A plain sense of what the next few weeks will hold, so the leader never wonders where this is going. One open question, *"What do you hope to see come of this group?"*, posed but not captured, because we invite reflection rather than extract it.

Then exactly one finishable thing: watch session one of the leader training, about six minutes. One completable step produces outsized steadiness.

## A gentle three-week arc

The shape maps to what a leader needs, in order.

- **Week one, structure and your role.** Steady footing through session one, then the language to invite an existing group well. Not "hit send," but how to start those conversations.
- **Week two, disposition.** Listening. *"I am a facilitator, not the answer."* This is where surrendered is built.
- **Week three, readiness.** Prepare for session one specifically. Calm, concrete, *"you are ready."*

The training never blasts out all at once. If a session is watched, we gently nudge the next, and repeat. The leader who is moving is not pushed. The leader who stalls gets a soft hand, and the silence is named without guilt.

## The two layers

The experience runs on two layers working in tandem.

The **deterministic layer** is the predetermined heartbeat: trigger-driven email and in-app delivery that surfaces the immediate next step and carries the confidence-building sequence.

The **conversation** is the non-deterministic layer. We have enough data to *guess* the next right step, but it is still a guess. The conversation gets us closer to the real need. It is an agentic tool with a bounded, legible scope, named by the action a leader is taking, never personified, never called "an AI." At the edge of its purview it points outward to people, a pastor or coordinator, rather than inward to itself.

> Email decides when. In-app is where. The conversation figures out what.

## How we would know it is working

Self-reported confidence after the first session is the clearest signal, defined in the same terms as the design intent. Alongside it: healthy movement on the signals the deterministic layer watches, without pressure being the cause, and conversations that hand the leader outward rather than keep them dependent.
