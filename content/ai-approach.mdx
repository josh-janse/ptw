# The place of AI

I want to be sketch out how AI is used in this experience and some of my thinking behind it, because the way it is used is an important part of the design.

> A tool named by what it helps you do, never a presence that asks to be related to.

## Named by action, never personified

Inside the experience there is one non-deterministic surface: the conversation. It is an agentic tool, and it is named by the action a leader is taking, never given a face, a name, or the word "AI." No avatar, no "typing" animation, no sense that someone is waiting on the other end. It is intended as a calm reading column that helps you take the next step and then gets out of the way.

## Bounded on purpose

The tool has a known, legible scope, made visible through named skills. It does a few things well rather than everything vaguely. That boundary is the point.

## Anti-dependency by design

This is the ethical center. The conversation is should be designed to make a leader more confident in their role and more surrendered in their disposition, not more reliant on software. At the edge of its scope it is meant to point outward, to a pastor or a coordinator and to durable resources, rather than inward to itself. It does not do the formation work for the leader. No generated session plans, no materials that stand in for engaging the course or the guide. The aim is to get closer to the real need, then hand the leader back to people.

## What I deliberately left out

In production I would ground the conversation in a vector database of the real training material, so its answers are anchored in the actual content rather than a system prompt and skills alone. I left that out here on purpose. It is not executable within the time of this exercise. So the prototype leans on a system prompt, a few bounded skills, and static data.
