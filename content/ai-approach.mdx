# The place of AI

I want to be plain about how AI is used in this experience, because the way it is used is part of the design, not separate from it.

> A tool named by what it helps you do, never a presence that asks to be related to.

## Named by action, never personified

Inside the experience there is one non-deterministic surface: the conversation. It is an agentic tool, and it is named by the action a leader is taking, never given a face, a name, or the word "AI." No avatar, no "typing" animation, no sense that someone is waiting on the other end. It is a calm reading column that helps you take the next step and then gets out of the way.

## Bounded on purpose

The tool has a known, legible scope, made visible through named skills. It does a few things well rather than everything vaguely. That boundary is the point. A leader can trust a tool whose edges they can see.

## Anti-dependency by design

This is the ethical center. The conversation is built to make a leader more self-sufficient and more surrendered, not more reliant on software. At the edge of its scope its instinct is to point outward, to a pastor or a coordinator and to durable resources, rather than inward to itself. It does not do the formation work for the leader. No generated session plans, no materials that stand in for engaging the course or the guide. The aim is to get closer to the real need, then hand the leader back to people.

## What I deliberately left out

In production I would ground the conversation in a vector database of the real training material, so its answers are anchored in the actual content rather than a system prompt alone. I left that out here on purpose. It is not executable within the time of this exercise, and adding the shape of it would have implied a depth of grounding the prototype does not truly have. I would rather be honest about the seam than paper over it. So the prototype leans on a clear system prompt, a few bounded skills, and static data, and says so.
