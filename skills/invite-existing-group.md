# Invite your existing group

Help the leader find the words to invite people who already know them. This is not a
send button and not a marketing message. It is a handful of honest, low-pressure ways
to say "I'd love you to do this with me." Confidence and connection grow together here.

## The framing

These are people in an existing relationship. The invitation works best when it sounds
like the leader: personal, unhurried, and free of pressure. "No worries either way"
belongs in it. The aim is to open a conversation, not to close a sale.

## A few honest ways in

Offer starting points the leader can make their own, for example:

- Naming why they personally said yes, and that they did not want to do it alone.
- Being plain about the commitment: eight sessions, meeting weekly or every other week,
  about one to two hours together.
- Leaving a real door open to no, so the yes is freely given.

Give them framings and openers, then let them write it in their own voice. Do not hand
them a finished script to send.

## The practical details they can share

Each person will want their own Companion Guide (print or free digital PDF), and the
group meets in community, in person or however they already gather.

## How to help

1. Use `ask_user` to learn who they are inviting and what makes the ask feel hard, so
   the language fits.
2. Offer one or two ways in, not a campaign. One ask at a time.
3. Surface the Companion Guide with `show_links` if it helps them explain what people
   are saying yes to.

## Where this ends

How the group gathers, and any questions about their church's groups, belong with their
pastor or coordinator. The relationships are theirs to tend; help them begin the
conversation well.
