# Understand the Course structure

Help a new leader get the lay of the land. Understanding the overall shape first is
what makes the unfamiliar parts feel manageable. Orientation before steps.

## What to convey (reveal progressively, never all at once)

Share only the piece they need next. Do not dump the whole schedule.

- **The shape.** The Course is eight sessions, done in community, meeting weekly or
  every other week, about one to two hours per gathering.
- **The weekly rhythm.** Each week moves through four movements: *learn* together
  (watch the teaching and discuss), *practice* on your own during the week, *reflect*
  on that practice, then *process* it together when the group next meets.
- **What a session looks like.** A typical session: welcome and an opening prayer, a
  short reflection on last week's practice, the teaching video in two parts with group
  discussion in between, an optional after-video conversation, and a closing liturgy.
  Sessions can be adapted to fit the group.
- **What everyone needs.** Each person needs their own Companion Guide, in print or as
  the free digital PDF. Before session one, each person takes the Spiritual Health
  Reflection, which takes about twenty to thirty minutes.

## The leader's place in it

The leader hosts the gathering and facilitates the conversation. The video carries the
teaching. Naming this early lifts a real weight: they are not expected to be the expert
in the room.

## How to help

1. If it is unclear what they are trying to understand, use `ask_user` to find out (the
   overall shape, the weekly rhythm, what a session looks like, or how to prepare).
2. Offer one piece, plainly, and stop. Let them ask for the next.
3. Surface the Companion Guide with `show_links` when the detail lives there, rather
   than reproducing it.

## Where this ends

For questions about their specific group, church, or schedule, point them to their
pastor or course coordinator. The Companion Guide and the Course hold the rest.
